4 · PREPARED FOR ANYTHING — the contingency playbook

If this fails… The move (rehearse it once)
Wi-Fi dies Switch to phone hotspot; if still down, use the deck PDF + the demo recording (both offline). Keep talking.
Live demo breaks Yasmine cuts to the screen recording within 20s. "Here's it running from this morning." No apology.
Projector/laptop won't connect Try the backup laptop, then the other adapter. Worst case: present from the laptop screen turned to the panel + printed slides. The story carries, not the pixels.
A speaker blanks 2-second pause is fine. The next line is on the printed script in front of them; or the timekeeper (Ziad) feeds the first word. Team, not solo.
Hostile / trick question Whoever owns the area answers, 30–45s, calm. "Here's what we know; here's the limit." Never two people at once. Never bluff.
"Did AI write this / are numbers real?" "Every figure is measured from the live data and regenerable — we applied our own One Law to our own report."
"Isn't the vaccine story fear-mongering?" "The first post is the fear-mongering; we show the documented consequence. Every number is verifiable — that's the whole point."
Running over time Ziad's silent signal at 12:00. Speakers after cut to their first + last sentence only. Protect Ziad's close — never get cut off mid-vision.
Someone is sick / absent Cross-train: each person can deliver the segment before and after theirs at 70%. Khaled re-assigns on the spot.
Panel interrupts mid-talk Answer briefly, "I'll expand on that in 60 seconds" if it's coming up — then continue. Don't lose the thread.
